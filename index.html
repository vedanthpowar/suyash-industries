VMC AMS 1060V

Technical Significance: Vertical Machining Centers (VMCs) are the backbone of our precision finishing. The AMS 1060V features a high-rigidity spindle and massive travel area, allowing us to perform high-speed milling, drilling, and tapping on large castings without repositioning the part, eliminating tolerance stacking errors.

CNC LMW LL20TL5 Turning Center

Technical Significance: Our advanced turning centers are crucial for producing perfectly concentric cylindrical components. With high rapid traverse rates and rigid slant-bed designs, they drastically reduce cycle times while maintaining micro-level accuracy.

CNC ACE Micro J300

Technical Significance: Compact but incredibly rigid, the ACE Micro series is optimized for high-volume batch production of smaller critical components. It ensures repeatable precision across thousands of cycles.

Organized Machining Floor

Technical Significance: A clean, well-lit, and systematically arranged 5S shop floor directly correlates to lower defect rates. Proper spacing between our VMC and CNC lines ensures safe material handling and efficient workflow.

×

Heavy Duty Casting Shed

Technical Significance: High-tonnage die casting requires immense infrastructure. Our factory features high ceilings for thermal ventilation, heavy-duty overhead cranes for mold changes, and reinforced flooring to absorb the shock of our 900 TON Yizumi machines locking.

Melting & Holding Furnaces

Technical Significance: Metallurgical integrity starts at the melt. Our temperature-controlled holding furnaces prevent oxide formation and gas porosity before the metal is ever injected into the die, ensuring superior mechanical properties in the final cast.

HPDC Machine Setup

Technical Significance: High Pressure Die Casting (HPDC) forces molten metal into a steel die under immense pressure. This rapid injection allows for extremely thin wall sections, smooth surface finishes, and near-net-shape components that require minimal secondary machining.

Hot Chamber vs Cold Chamber

Technical Significance: We maintain both Hot Chamber (up to 138 TON) and Cold Chamber (up to 280 TON) machines. This versatility allows us to cast everything from low-melting-point Zinc alloys in rapid cycles, to high-strength Aluminum alloys for structural automotive parts.

×

NDT Dye Penetrant Testing Lab

Technical Significance: Visual inspection isn't enough for safety-critical components. We use Non-Destructive Dye Penetrant (NDT-DP) testing to draw bright red dye into microscopic surface cracks or porosity, exposing structural flaws that the human eye cannot see. This guarantees zero-defect deliveries.

Precision Gauge Setup

Technical Significance: Our inspection room utilizes high-end Digital Height Gauges and customized Air Gauges. Air gauging uses pneumatic pressure to measure bore diameters down to micron-level accuracy without scratching the machined surface.
